About Me

I am a Ph.D. student in the computer science department at CU Boulder advised by Claire Monteleoni

I like spending time with Neural Networks!


My research is focused on unsupervised learning with a focus on spatiotemporal representation learning. I am also passionate about applying machine learning methods in computational photography as well as in scientific domains like climate science, in order to study how large-scale spatiotemporal data with complex correlations and high dimensionality affect the representation and thus the generalization of machine learning models.

In past, I’ve worked with Prof. Auroop Ganguly on understanding the trade-off and hence, the optimality between deeper versus shallower MC-dropout based networks on approximating noisy polynomials. I've worked with Prof. Udit Bhatia on the task of generating high resolution climate change projections using spatial-recurrent models. I've also worked with Prof. Shanmuganathan Raman on the task of generating High Dynamic Range(HDR) video frames from Low Dynamic Range(LDR) with the focus temporally stability .
